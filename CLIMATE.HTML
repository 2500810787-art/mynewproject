Primary Causes

Fossil Fuels

Coal, oil, and gas combustion releases CO₂ & methane, trapping heat. 75% of global emissions come from energy sector.

Deforestation

Forests act as carbon sinks. Loss of Amazon & tropical forests accelerates warming and disrupts rainfall patterns.

Agriculture

Livestock emits methane; fertilizers release nitrous oxide — potent greenhouse gases. Land use change adds pressure.

Feedback Loops

Melting permafrost releases methane, creating self-reinforcing cycles that amplify warming beyond human emissions.

Cascading Effects

Extreme Weather

Heatwaves, wildfires, hurricanes, and floods are becoming more frequent & intense due to disrupted climate systems.

Sea Level Rise

Melting ice sheets & thermal expansion threaten coastal cities — over 800 million people at risk by 2050.

Biodiversity Collapse

1 million species face extinction; coral reefs bleach, ecosystems lose resilience.

Human Health

Heat stress, respiratory diseases from wildfires, and infectious diseases expanding ranges.

Global Temperature Anomaly (1880–2023)

Data (relative to 1951-1980 baseline) shows unprecedented warming in recent decades. Source: NASA/GISS.

Tangible Solutions & Action

Renewable Energy

Solar, wind, and hydro are cheaper than ever. Rapid transition to clean energy could cut emissions by 70% by 2050.

Circular Economy

Reducing waste, regenerating nature, carbon capture, and reforestation are key to net-zero pathways.

Electrify Transport

EVs, public transit, biking infrastructure — transport accounts for 15% of global emissions.

Policy & Justice

Paris Agreement, carbon pricing, and climate finance for vulnerable nations drive systemic change.
